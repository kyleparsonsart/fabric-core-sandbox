## Key Callouts

**Above-scope work on Colors Northstar**
The color ramp expansion from 8 to 11 shades, with a contrast bell-curve for better light/dark surface transitions, was not in the original scope. This was a necessary investment to build a more UI-ambitious Token Northstar and was prioritized accordingly. It adds ~2–3 days to the Colors deliverable but sets the components pipeline up on a much stronger foundation.

**Components pipeline is sequenced — not blocked**
The Input/Select Box/Text Area and Radio/Checkbox/Avatar groups have not started exploration yet, but this is intentional sequencing. Color token updates must stabilize before component explorations begin to avoid rework. Once Colors wraps (~4 days), approximately 6 days of component work flows through in sequence. Accordion/Card/Popover/Modal/Drawer is queued on bandwidth and is not color-dependent.

**Concept Car Support deprioritized**
The Concept Car project was intentionally deprioritized this quarter to protect bandwidth for TNS completion and the expanded color ramp work. This was a deliberate trade-off. 10 days of Concept Car work remains and is not currently scoped for Q1 completion.

**Token Analytics Dashboard needs early action**
The Token Analytics Dashboard can run in parallel with other work, but depends on external metric collection from Supriya and/or Greg. This outreach needs to begin soon to avoid the timeline compressing at the end of the quarter. The plan is to present findings via the TNS sandbox site.

## Overall Assessment

The Token Northstar deliverables are on track to meet the May 1 deadline for all in-scope work. The critical path runs through Colors Northstar → Components → Figma Tokens Cleanup. With ~28 working days of work remaining and ~28 working days until the deadline, the schedule is tight but achievable. The primary risks are revision cycles in components work and the need to kick off Token Analytics Dashboard coordination with stakeholders immediately.
